Research

(2022-2024) - Improvement and Pedagogical Analysis of Inclusive 3D Concrete Teaching Materials for Visually Impaired People for Learning Information Systems Modeling

In the realm of education, the scarcity of inclusive teaching materials for individuals with visual impairments remains a challenge, especially in disciplines like Software Engineering, which heavily rely on visual diagrams. These diagrams, such as Class Diagrams and Use Case Diagrams, are often inaccessible to visually impaired students due to their complex graphical nature. To address this issue, the research focused on enhancing inclusive teaching materials for visually impaired individuals, particularly in Software Engineering and Database concepts. The approach involved tactile adaptations of UML diagrams. The research began with a theoretical foundation and analysis of existing materials, followed by refinement, printing, and construction of the improved material. After implementation and feedback from a visually impaired student, the material was finalized and consisted of tactile diagram pieces, auxiliary tables, and a usage guide. Overall, the developed material showed significant pedagogical potential and contributes to the inclusion of visually impaired individuals in educational settings.
